The History of Kashmir
  1. 4 January, 1990: Srinagar-based newspaper Aftab released a message, threatening all Hindus to leave Kashmir immediately.
  2. 14 April, 1990: Another Srinagar based newspaper Al-safa republished the same warning.
  3. Walls were pasted with posters with threatening messages to all Kashmiris to strictly follow Islamic rules.
  4. This included abidance by the Islamic dress code, a prohibition on alcohol, cinemas, strict restrictions on women. and video parlors
  5. Offices buildings, shops, and establishments were colored green as a sign of Islamic rule.
  6. Shops, factories, temples and homes of Kashmiri Hindus were burned or destroyed.
  7. Threatening posters were posted on doors of Hindus asking them to leave Kashmir immediately.
  8. During the middle of the night of 18 and 19 January, a blackout took place in the Kashmir Valley where electricity was cut except in mosques[citation needed] which broadcast divisive and inflammatory messages, asking for a purge of Kashmiri Hindus.
